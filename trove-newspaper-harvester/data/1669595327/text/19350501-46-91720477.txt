 SPEEDY AIR MAILS. Sydney Within Seven Days of London. SIR FREDERICK WILLIAMSON'S HOPES. LONDON, Monday. - Sir Froderick Williamson, interviewed by the Aus tralian Press Association on his return from Australia to-day, expressed gratifi cation with the Commonwealth's adher ence in princi ple to the Imperial air mail scheme, which he still hoped would be operating in 1937, bringing Sydney within seven days of London. Technical negotiations, ho said, wore proceeding between the British and Australian Post Offices and Treasury officials. Sir Frederick Williamson hoped that agreement upon outstanding financial problems would be expedited, in view of time time needed to provide the now fleet of aircraft required to operate high speed services. The Australian Ministers, apparent ly, are awaiting the arrival of Captain Johnson, before raising the matter at Whitehall.
