 SUCCESS OF A SYDNEY JOCKEY FOUR WINNERS IN A DAY. LONDON, September 11. Frank Wootton, the Sydney jockey has had another lucky day in England - Wootton rode four winners at the Doncaster races yesterday In May last Frank Wootton rode three winners on the one day at the Doncaster meeting Frank Wootton is the eldest son of Richard Wootton, the well known owner and trainer at one time of Randwick, but now following his occupation in England. He is now 14 years old and commenced riding in public in South Africa three years ago, his first mount being a winning one On his return from South Africa, in 1905, the lad could not get a license in Australia, owing to his years, but he rode Fabric in work prior to the success of the Bill of Portland horse in Tattersall's Cup, 1906, at Rand- wick Subsequently the lad proceeded to England with his father, and during that season he rode in 67 races, piloting 18 winners, 11 seconds, and six thirds Last season his record was 282 mounts 39 wins, 37 seconds and 27 thirds, while this season he has already steered a number of winners, including Lord Derby's Glaces in the Queen's Prize at Kempton park and in the Chester Cup, and Colonel Ten wickets Fan Faith in the Richmond park Easter Handicap at Kempton park, and Mr J B Thorney croft's Damage in the Crawford Plate at Newmarket on whom he put in some brilliant work towards the finish, beating the favourite by a neck a
