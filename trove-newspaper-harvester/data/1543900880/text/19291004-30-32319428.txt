 SYDNEY TO PERTH. A NEW PROJECT. Air and Rail Journey of 35 Hours. MELBOURNE, Oct. 3.— To demonstrate what can be accomplished by the co ordination of aeroplane and railway ser vices Australian Aerial Services, Ltd., and the Commonwealth Railways Department will complete arrangements soon for a combined air and rail journey across Aus tralia from Sydney to Perth. The whole journey is estimated to occupy only 35 hours. It will be possible to take break fast in Sydney one day and dinner iu Perth the next day. .It is not intended that the demonstra tion should be in the nature of a_ record breaking stunt, as it is desired to afford the passengers the greatest comfort possible. There will be no night flying. The schedule provides that the middle part of the journey from Port Augusta to Ooldea will take place at night so that during the period of darkness the travel ler will be in a well-equipped train, with hot and cold showers, a dining saloon and sleeping apartments. Xo part of the flight will be over water or dangerous country, and to ensure this provision the route has been carefully surveyed. The - total distance to be 'covered Js 2,187 miles and against the 35 hours by combined air and raiL travel, the train journey for the whole distance takes. 108 hours. By steamer the distance is 2,440 miles and the time taken is 264 hours. The time-table for the first journey is: Leave Sydney by aeroplane in the morn ing and arrive at Port- Augusta at 5.30 o clock on the same day. Take the Great Western express from Port Augusta at 6 o clock that evening and arrive at Ooldea at 6.oO o'clock on the following morn ing. Leave Ooldea by aeroplane halfan hour later and reach Perth at 0.30 o'clock in the_ evening. Business men from Victoria and New Ssouth Wales will be among the first pas sengers to make the trip, the first class single fare for -which is £27/18/.
